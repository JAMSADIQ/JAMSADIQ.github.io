I was a postdoctoral research candidate at IGFAE at USC from November 2020 till October 2022. I have complted my PhD from Rochester Institute of Technology. My research focus on Gravitational Wave data analysis. Specifically I worked on rated and population studies of binary black holes in the universe. I also work on PyGRB search which a modelled search for gravitational waves involving concident gamma ray observation. I developed a code on hybridizing numerical relativity and post Newtonian waveforms for precessing binary black holes. In my PhD I also worked on numerical relativity where I developed a method to test accuracy of an anlytical spacetime for binary black holes by comparing it with numerical relativity evolution. the accuracy numerical simulations of the inspiral and merger phases of orbiting black hole binaries. In particular, I am interested in exploring the extreme limits of general relativity in the regime of near-maximal intrinsic angular momentum. In addition to probing the peculiarities of nonlinear gravity theory, I plan to generate astrophysical gravitational waveforms for use in gravitational wave detection experiments.
