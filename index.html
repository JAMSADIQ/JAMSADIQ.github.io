Homepage

I am a postdoctoral research candidate at SISSA at Trieste, Italy since February 2023. I was a postdoctoral researcher at IGFAE at USC Santiago de Compostela, Spain from November 2020 till February 2023. I have completed my PhD from Rochester Institute of Technology.

My research focuses on Gravitational Wave data analysis, specifically on rates and population studies of binary black holes in the universe. I also work on PyGRB search, a modelled search for gravitational waves involving coincident gamma-ray detections. I developed a code for hybridizing numerical relativity and post-Newtonian waveforms for precessing binary black holes. During my PhD, I also acquired skills in numerical relativity simulation of binary black holes. In my research project, I developed a method to test the accuracy of an analytical spacetime for binary black holes by comparing its analytical evolution with numerical relativity evolution.
